## Decision Tree

### If the product must run in a browser

Build a dashboard, consent flow, map UI, calibration workflow, and WebSocket ingest. Treat RF data as external.

### If the product must use a phone with no root

Use the phone as:

- UI and consent surface.
- IMU/orientation source.
- Cooperative traffic generator.
- Android RTT initiator when APs support it.

Do not promise passive through-wall sensing.

### If the product can include cheap hardware

Use ESP32 CSI nodes first. They are cheap, deployable, and easy to isolate from phone OS limitations.

### If the product is a research prototype

Use PicoScenes, Nexmon CSI, Wi-BFI, or SDR depending on whether the target is raw CSI, phone-adjacent BFI, or passive radar.

## Capability Ladder

1. RSSI trend: "something changed" near the AP.
2. CSI/BFI presence: occupancy and motion zones after room calibration.
3. CSI/BFI activity: walking/sitting/gesture classes in known environment.
4. CSI/BFI vitals: respiration under still-body assumptions and close calibration.
5. Pose/dense pose: requires multi-antenna data, paired labels or strong pretrained models, and careful validation.
6. Through-wall claims: signal-dependent and highly constrained; treat as experimental.

## Why Phone Antenna Placement Still Matters

Even if the app cannot read raw CSI, phone antenna placement matters for:

- RTT ranging bias and orientation sensitivity.
- Beamforming feedback generated by the phone.
- Cooperative-transmitter experiments.
- Near-field hand/body loading.
- Multi-antenna spatial signatures if a rooted/firmware path exists.

But placement alone does not unlock sensing. The missing piece is access to the PHY measurements.
